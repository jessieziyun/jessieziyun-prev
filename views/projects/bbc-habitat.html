BBC Habitat


Data Visualisation, Augmented Reality

D&AD New Blood Awards Yellow Pencil Winner 2020.

In a world increasingly saturated with data, how can we harness it for good? Climate change is the most pressing issue of our generation, yet it can often feel distant and abstract.

BBC Habitat is an app that visualises users' carbon footprint as a personal Habitat. The information that the app provides helps users understand how their actions affect the environment, while the immersve experience of augmented reality brings people into the habitats at risk to grant a feeling of connection to the natural world.

Insight

16-24 year olds are concerned about climate change and want to change their behaviours to reduce their impact on the environment but find it difficult to keep consistently motivated.

Strategy

Provide people with information about their carbon footprint and environmental news in an engaging way to support them in making meaningful change.

Role: Concept, Co-Creative Direction, Audio Visual
In collaboration with Thomas Bugg, Jann Choy and Betty Lu
